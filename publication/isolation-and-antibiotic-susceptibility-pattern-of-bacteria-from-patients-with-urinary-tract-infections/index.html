Isolation and Antibiotic Susceptibility Pattern of Bacteria from Patients with Urinary Tract Infections

Md. Jubayer Hossain, Abul Kalam Azad, Jannatul Ferdous, Md. Shahadat Bin Shahid
January 2023 Antibiotic Resistance and Prevention(ARP)
Source Document 36th BSM Annual Conference

Abstract

Introduction: Antibiotic resistance (AR) in urinary tract infection (UTI) disease is a global public health problem specially in developing countries. Regular surveillance about antimicrobial susceptibility patterns of UTIs causing bacteria are crucial for selecting appropriate treatment. Therefore, the present study aimed to analyze the antimicrobial susceptibility patterns of UTIs causing bacteria isolated from patients at Mughda Medical College Hospital, Dhaka, Bangladesh. Methods: A one-year retrospective study was done at the Mugdha Medical College Hospital in Dhaka (January 2019 to December 2020). The study comprised the clinical information and laboratory findings of individuals with a positive urine culture (105 CFU/mL urine). Patients were divided into four age groups such as infants and adolescents (1-18 years), young adults (18-33 years), late adults (33-50 years) and elderly (50-95 years). Antibiotic sensitivity assay was performed following standard Kirby Bauer method against 28 most common antibiotics. Study Results: Total 243 urine reports were analyzed, and our result showed that Escherichia coli was the most prevalent (65.84%) uropathogen in community and hospital acquired UTIs followed by Klebsiella spp (12.34 %), Enterococcus spp (8.23 %), Streptococcus spp (6.58 %), Proteus spp (2.88 %), Pseudomonas spp (2.46 %), and Staphylococcus spp (1.64 %). Interestingly, Proteus spp was most prevalent in infants and adolescents (86%) while Pseudomonas spp was most prevalent in elderly populations (83%). Most bacteria were isolated from females (82 %). The highest prevalence was found in elderly groups (34%) followed by young adults (24%), late adults (22%) and infants and adolescents (18%). In case of antibiotic sensitivity, we found that E. coli, Klebsiella spp, Enterococcus spp, Streptococcus spp, Proteus spp, Pseudomonas spp, Staphylococcus spp were resistant (above 90% resistance) against 2, 7, 2, 2, 10, 2, 2 antibiotics respectively. While, E. coli, Klebsiella spp, Enterococcus spp, Streptococcus spp, Proteus spp, Pseudomonas spp, Staphylococcus spp were sensitive (above 90% sensitivity) against 9, 10, 17, 2, 1, 13, 17 antibiotics respectively. Conclusion: It can be concluded that elderly people are most vulnerable aged group and E. coli was most prevalent organism for UTIs in Mugdha Medical College Hospital area. Our study results suggested that appropriate antibiotic selection is essential for effective treatment of UTIs in different age group people.
